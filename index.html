Why home

Familiar is functional.

The house someone has lived in for decades is a map their memory already holds. The kitchen where she raised her kids. The lanai where he watches the rain come in. The photographs that tell a person who they are.

86% of adults 65 and older say they want to stay in their own homes as long as possible. AARP, 2018

Home isn't sentimental. For an aging body, and especially an aging mind, familiar surroundings do quiet work every day: orientation, confidence, routine. Research on moving older adults points the same direction again and again. Relocation is a setback, and for people with memory changes, an unfamiliar place commonly deepens confusion.

Care at home, done well, holds people steady. In studies of nurse-coordinated care in the community, people tended to hold or improve while comparable nursing-home residents declined. Staying home isn't the compromise. It's often the better medicine.
