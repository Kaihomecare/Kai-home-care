      Why home, really

      The cheaper option isn't the better one

      A care home costs less than staying put. But a care home doesn't have the kitchen where she raised four kids, the lanai where he still watches the rain come in off the mountains, or the wall of photographs that tells a person who they are every time they walk past it.

      Home isn't sentimental. For an aging body and especially an aging mind, familiar is functional. The house someone has lived in for decades is a map their memory already holds: where the bathroom is at 2 a.m., which stair creaks, where the coffee lives. Take that map away and the mind has to rebuild it from nothing, right when rebuilding is hardest. That's why the research on moving older adults, especially those with any memory loss, points the same direction again and again.

      86%WANT TO STAY
      People are telling you where they want to beIn a national survey, the overwhelming majority of adults 65 and older said they want to remain in their own homes for as long as possible. This is the most common wish your loved one has almost certainly already voiced.AARP, 2018.
      Moving frail elders tends to set them backResearchers describe a well-documented pattern, sometimes called relocation stress or transfer trauma, in which the health of a frail older person can decline with each move to a new setting. For people with dementia, an unfamiliar environment commonly worsens confusion, agitation, and disorientation, because familiar surroundings are doing quiet work to keep them oriented.Reviews of relocation and dementia outcomes, The Gerontologist and related literature.
      Care at home, done right, holds people steadyIn studies of community-based long-term care coordinated by nurses, participants tended to hold steady or improve on key measures, while matched nursing-home residents tended to decline. The care that keeps someone home, when it's real care, isn't a compromise on health. It often protects it.Aging in Place research, University of Missouri Sinclair School of Nursing.

      This is the whole idea behind Kai. Bring capable, watchful people into the home so the home can keep doing what no facility can: hold a life together. Your caregiver becomes another familiar face at the table. Family still visits on their own time, in their own way. The photographs stay on the wall.

      Straight talk

      What we do, and what we don't

      Kai Home Care provides non-medical homemaker and personal care. Our caregivers help with daily living: bathing, dressing, meals, mobility, companionship, and the running of a home. They do not provide skilled nursing, wound care, injections, or administer medications. Medication support means reminders only. If your family needs skilled medical care, we'll say so plainly and help you find it. And we'll be honest about one more thing: good care reduces risk, it cannot eliminate it. No agency can promise that no accident will ever happen. What we promise is trained people, watchful eyes, and a plan built to lower the odds.
