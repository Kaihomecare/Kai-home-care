    Six questions to ask any agency

    Print this page and take it with you. Any agency worth hiring will answer all six without flinching.

    • Are your caregivers W-2 employees or 1099 contractors?Kai: employees. We carry the payroll taxes, workers' compensation, and liability. A contractor injured in your home can look to you as the employer. Our employees never can.
    • Are you licensed and insured?Kai: yes, both.
    • Who covers a caregiver injured on the job in my home?Kai: our workers' compensation policy. Never your homeowner's insurance.
    • What happens when a caregiver calls out sick?Kai: we staff the backup. The visit still happens.
    • Can we request a different caregiver?Kai: any time, no awkwardness, no explanation needed.
    • Who supervises the care?Kai: a coordinator who knows your family by name and answers the phone.

    Straight talk

    What we do, and what we don't

    Kai Home Care provides non-medical homemaker and personal care. Our caregivers help with daily living: bathing, dressing, meals, mobility, companionship, and the running of a home. They do not provide skilled nursing, wound care, injections, or administer medications. Medication support means reminders only. If your family needs skilled medical care, we'll say so plainly and help you find it. And we'll be honest about one more thing: good care reduces risk, it cannot eliminate it. No agency can promise that no accident will ever happen. What we promise is trained people, watchful eyes, and a plan built to lower the odds.
