The job, honestly

What we offer, and what we ask.

  • $24–27 per hour, published because we don't play games with pay.
  • W-2 employment. We pay the payroll taxes and carry workers' compensation. You're covered if you're hurt on the job. Agencies that 1099 you leave that on you.
  • Steady, matched clients. You see the same families, visit after visit. No daily gig roulette.
  • A coordinator who answers. Something wrong in the home? You call, a real person on Oʻahu picks up.
  • Four-hour minimum visits. No two-hour drives for one-hour shifts.

The work is real. You'll help people bathe, dress, and move safely. You'll cook, clean, drive to appointments, and sit with someone whose family can't be there. Some clients have memory loss and will ask you the same question nine times. Some days are heavy.

If that paragraph made you tired, this isn't your job, and that's fine. If it made you feel useful, keep reading, because families across this island are waiting for someone exactly like you.

We hire for character and reliability first. Credentials matter, but we've trained skills into people with heart far more often than we've found heart in people with resumes. Recent nurse aide graduates and experienced family caregivers are welcome to apply.
